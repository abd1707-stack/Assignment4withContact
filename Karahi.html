Karahi

Karahi is a classic Pakistani dish named after the wok-like vessel in which it is cooked. It can be made with chicken, mutton, or beef, and is known for its bold, spicy flavors. The meat is cooked in a base of tomatoes, green chilies, garlic, and ginger, along with a variety of spices like cumin, coriander, and garam masala. The dish is often finished off with a dollop of butter or ghee to add richness. Karahi is traditionally cooked and served in the same pot, and it is usually eaten with naan or rice. Some variations include adding bell peppers or cream for extra richness.
