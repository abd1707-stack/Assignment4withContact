Haleem

Haleem is a slow-cooked, savory porridge made from a mixture of wheat, barley, lentils, and meat (typically beef, chicken, or mutton). The meat is cooked until it breaks down into shreds, and then blended with the grains and spices to form a thick, hearty paste. The dish is cooked for hours on a low flame, allowing the flavors to develop deeply. It is traditionally garnished with fried onions, chopped coriander, lemon wedges, and a drizzle of ghee for extra richness. Haleem is a popular dish during Ramadan, as it is filling and nutritious, providing energy after long hours of fasting.
