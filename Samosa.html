Samosa

Samosas are deep-fried pastries that are crispy on the outside and filled with a spicy mixture of potatoes, peas, and sometimes minced meat (beef, chicken, or mutton). The outer shell is made from thin dough that is folded into a triangular shape and sealed before being fried to a golden brown. The filling is flavored with a variety of spices, including cumin, coriander, garam masala, and chili powder. Samosas are a popular street food and snack in Pakistan, often served with mint chutney or tamarind sauce. They are a common appetizer during festive occasions like Eid and weddings.
