Peshawari Chapli Kebab

The Peshawari Chapli Kebab is a regional variation of the traditional chapli kebab, originating from Peshawar in the north-western part of Pakistan. Made from ground beef or mutton, the meat is mixed with ingredients like onions, tomatoes, green chilies, coriander seeds, and spices such as garam masala. The kebabs are shaped into thick patties and fried until crispy on the outside. The key difference between regular chapli kebabs and the Peshawari version is the addition of more spices and herbs, which make these kebabs particularly fragrant and flavorful.
