Nihari

Nihari is a rich and hearty stew, originally from the Mughal era, made by simmering beef, mutton, or chicken in a gravy of onions, ginger, garlic, and a blend of spices like turmeric, cinnamon, and garam masala. The meat is cooked for hours, allowing the flavors to meld and the meat to become tender and melt-in-the-mouth. Traditionally, it is served with naan or steamed rice, and garnished with fresh coriander, fried onions, and a squeeze of lemon. Nihari is often eaten as a breakfast dish, particularly in Lahore and Karachi, and is known for being a comforting and indulgent meal, perfect for cold mornings.
